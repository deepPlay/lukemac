I'm a designer.


I don't mean that I wear black rimmed glasses and tight jeans, or only listen to music you've never heard of with huge headphones, or spend all weekend loitering in a cafe with my macbook. I mean that I look at problems using design thinking, an approach characterised by rationality, creativity and empathy for the context of the problem. In practical terms, this means an emphasis on workability in solutions, fearless exploration of ideas, and critically, a lot of communication in every language at one's disposal: words, pictures, numbers, metaphor. After all, real-world projects are usually so-called 'wicked problems' with evolving or ill-defined criteria - and design thinking is more real-world than theory; more hard hat than lab coat; more Lego than Barbie.

I'm Luke Mac. My jeans fit me well and I can't hold my coffee. Let's make something wicked.
